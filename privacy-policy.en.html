Privacy Policy

Jesus Disciple Movement Bangkok Church (“the Church”) respects the privacy and personal data of all visitors to this website. This document is intended to explain how personal data is collected, used, disclosed, and protected in accordance with the Personal Data Protection Act B.E. 2562 (PDPA) and related laws.

1. Personal Data We Collect

The Church may collect the following personal data from you:

  • Name
  • Email address or phone number
  • Messages or information you submit through contact forms, prayer request forms, or other communication channels
  • Technical data such as IP address, device type, browser, and website usage data

2. Purpose of Data Collection and Use

The Church collects and uses your personal data only as necessary for the following purposes:

  • To contact you and respond to your inquiries
  • For pastoral care and prayer responses
  • To inform you about church news, activities, or operations
  • To improve the quality and user experience of the website
  • For statistical analysis of website usage

3. Principles of Personal Data Processing

The Church processes personal data in accordance with legal, fair, and transparent principles. Personal data is collected and used only as necessary for the stated purposes and will not be used for other purposes without the consent of the data subject.

4. Cookies and Analytics Tools

The website may use cookies and analytics tools such as Google Analytics to collect anonymous statistical data. Users can choose to accept or reject non-essential cookies through the cookie banner on the website.

5. Disclosure of Personal Data

The Church does not sell, trade, or disclose personal data to third parties, except in the following cases:

  • As required by law
  • With the consent of the data subject
  • When necessary for the operation of the website or related systems

6. Data Retention and Security

The Church retains personal data only as necessary for the stated purposes and implements appropriate technical and administrative measures to prevent unauthorized access, use, or disclosure of personal data.

7. Data Subject Rights

You have the following rights under the Personal Data Protection Act:

  • The right to access and obtain a copy of your personal data
  • The right to correct and update your personal data
  • The right to request deletion or destruction of your personal data
  • The right to suspend or object to the processing of your personal data
  • The right to withdraw consent for personal data processing at any time
  • The right to lodge a complaint with the supervisory authority if you believe that the processing of your data is not in accordance with the law
